Colour here always refers to its colour in PPL (Plane Polarised Light)

Most minerals will appear some sort of gray or yellow on its own in PPL.

However, you can see the subtle differences in colour and tint when you compare it to another mineral side-by-side.

The way the mineral changes brightness in PPL as you rotate the stage.

Although often lumped together, it is different from pleochroism. Pleochroism is the change in colour when you rotate the stage and bireflectance is the change in brightness. A mineral is often both pleochroic and bireflectant.

More on anisotropy

Softer minerals will appear lower, sunken down, sometimes more scratched due to polishing, compared to the harder mineral next to it. And vice versa.

It's a similar visual effect to relief for minerals seen under transmitted light sections.
